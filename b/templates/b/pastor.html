Week of April 12, 2021

Understanding Kingdom Wealth
and Prosperity (Part 1)

"Let those who favor my righteous cause and have pleasure in my uprightness shout for joy and be glad and say continually, Let the Lord be magnified, Who takes pleasure in the prosperity of His servant."   (Psalm 35:27 ► (AMP))

Dearly Beloved,
Supernatural turnaround greetings to you in the name of Jesus and welcome to again to April 2021: our month of fearful testimonies. Last week, the body of Christ celebrated the resurrection of Jesus - the singular event that sets Christianity apart from every other religion. Jesus' sacrifice on the cross of Calvary secured access to infinite supply for every believer so this month we will be exploring one great gift that was contained in that redemption package in a series titled: Understanding Kingdom Wealth & Prosperity.

Of all the Scriptural themes, none evokes such fierce debate as that of Biblical prosperity. There is also probably no other theological subject that is so misunderstood so I'll like to kick off this series by saying that Kingdom prosperity is the ability to use the power of God to meet any need in the spiritual, mental, physical, financial and social realms. It is having enough of God’s supply to complete His instructions for your life, and enough of His provisions to accomplish commands and expectations.

There is no true prosperity apart from God (Haggai 2:8). Every category of prosperity; whether it is spiritual, temporal, historical, or even dying prosperity has God as its true source. According to Philippians 4:11-13, 17-19, it is the will of God to prosper His people. It is His will to see us joyful, happy, blessed, healthy, and having all of our needs met according to HIS RICHES and HIS GLORY and in this prophetic season, God the Father is pouring into the corporate body of Christ supernatural wealth and prosperity to advance the kingdom of God, to meet the needs of the saints, and to purposefully enjoy unto the praises of His glory!

Kingdom wealth is part of our covenant benefits and God wants all your needs met abundantly. In His death, Jesus took upon himself all our sins, all our, sickness and disease, all our lack and poverty, and death itself. In their place, He gave us all that was His: life, health and blessing so as I sign off this week, it is my prayer that beginning this week, God will permanently break the curse of poverty in your life, lift your thoughts from lack, limitation and present difficulties; and create for you an opening of abundant supernatural supply in Jesus name. Amen!

Let God be true. . .

Moses Ajayi
Resident Pastor
